Overview

Rain's unlimited data products are often used by families, roommates, couples and shared households. While one person typically owns the account, the cost is frequently shared informally through bank transfers or cash.

The goal of Chip In was to formalise this behaviour by allowing anyone to contribute directly towards another customer's Rain account through a secure, self-service experience.

Rather than introducing account sharing or complex billing changes, the solution created a simple contribution model that fits naturally into Rain's existing ecosystem.

The Challenge.

Rain customers already shared the cost of data. The problem was that this happened outside the platform.

Typical scenarios included:

  • Students sharing accommodation
  • Families paying for parents or children
  • User journeys
  • Couples splitting internet costs
  • Friends sharing household WiFi

These arrangements relied on:

  • EFTs
  • Cash
  • Reminders
  • Trust

The opportunity was to create a digital experience that made contributing effortless while keeping ownership of the account with the original customer.

The scheduling experience. Walk scheduling needed to be achievable in under thirty seconds on a phone. Two entry points were designed: a "Book Walk" button on each dog card for volunteers who already knew which dog they wanted, and a Schedule Walk wizard in the bottom nav for multi-dog group bookings. Three fixed daily time slots replaced a freeform picker to constrain complexity. A recurrence engine allowed walks to be booked up to twelve weeks ahead. The My Walks section gave each volunteer a personal history — hours volunteered, completed walks, upcoming sessions — serving both as motivation and as an administrative audit trail. The platform was built within Google AI Studio using a Gemini-powered workflow, compressed into days rather than weeks, and deployed to Google Cloud Run as a containerised static build, or a progressive web app.
